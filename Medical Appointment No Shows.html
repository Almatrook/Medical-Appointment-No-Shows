Introduction¶

Healthcare expenses have cost a fortune. The average hourly pay of a physician is considered high regardless of the different salary structure around the globe. The cost of that time slot remains the same whether or not a patient missed his/her medical appointment.

In this project, the data associated with scheduled patient appointments will be analyzed. In particular, the findings from trends among the patients who no show and the others who attended a doctor appointment. This project aims to understand the possibility of predicting patients who fail to appear in a medical's appointment.

Conclusions¶

Project Limitations¶

In this project, four independent variables were compared to one dependent variable. However, there are many independent variables need to be studied and could be effective in final results and these are the following:

  1. Neighbourhood column describes the appointment location. From initial observation, I noticed that the sample is lack of two important elements:

    a. Patient location and how far is it from appointment place.

    b. Patient's livelihood in this neighbourhood.

  1. Scholarship column Indicates if the patient part of the Brazilian social welfare program or not, which I think studying this factor will be described the patient no-show behaviour when receiving a free service.
  1. The patient health conditions could be a factor affecting their attendance. One of the important factor is handicapped people and their obstacles to reach the appointment because of mobility difficulties. Also, alcoholism causes discipline problems.
  1. Gaps between scheduled visit time and appointment day could be affecting patient dissatisfaction and his/her obligation appointment.

Summary¶

The most important findings are:

  1. Tuesday and Wednesday are most weekdays for patients to show up in doctor's appointment even though there are no significant difference with other days. As result of the number of approved appointment, there are 30 visits on Saturday and none on Sunday.
  1. Female has the highest percentage of missing their medical appointment with almost twice than male.
  1. Despite receiving an SMS notification has increased patients obligation on their appointment by triple time than patients who Ignored notification, there are more than half of people who visited the doctor without receiving SMS. The most dominant percentage of them was female.
  1. The most classification of patients age who shows up are young adults(18 - 40) by women higher than men.
  1. For all studied independent variables, the distributions of patients attendance look similar. However, patient gender is more prominent than others.
